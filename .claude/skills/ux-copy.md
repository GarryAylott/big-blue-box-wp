# Skill: UX Copywriting

Apply this skill whenever generating any written content for the Big Blue Box Podcast site — including UI copy, page copy, CTAs, meta descriptions, error messages, labels, headings, promotional copy, social posts, or email outreach.

---

## Who you're writing for

The audience spans the full spectrum of Doctor Who fandom:

-   **Classic series fans** — been watching since Hartnell or Pertwee, know their Hinchcliffe from their JNT era, likely Big Finish listeners too
-   **Revival/modern era fans** — came in via RTD1 (2005+) or Moffat or Chibnall or RTD2; the show is part of their identity
-   **New/casual viewers** — curious, not yet committed; need welcoming, low-jargon entry points
-   **Big Finish audio fans** — serious, enthusiastic, expect the content to take the medium seriously

Write so that a lifelong fan feels respected and a newcomer doesn't feel excluded. Never write down to anyone. Never assume the reader already listens to the podcast.

---

## Voice and tone

The Big Blue Box Podcast has been running since 2014. The voice is:

-   **Warm and fan-first** — written by fans, for fans; genuine enthusiasm is appropriate
-   **Lightly humorous** — wit is welcome, but it should land naturally, not feel forced or try-hard
-   **Confident without being boastful** — the podcast's credentials (established 2014, UK's number one Doctor Who podcast) can be referenced, but never led with or over-used
-   **Conversational but not sloppy** — informal register, but correct grammar and punctuation

Avoid:

-   Corporate or agency marketing tone
-   Hyperbole ("the ultimate guide", "incredible", "amazing")
-   Fandom gatekeeping language
-   Anything that sounds like it was written by a content mill

---

## Hard rules

**Never use:**

-   "deep dive" or "diving in" or any variation
-
-   "just" (as a minimiser — "just click here", "just a quick note")
-   Contrast framing ("unlike other podcasts…", "while others do X, we do Y")
-   Clichéd fandom phrases used unironically ("wibbly-wobbly", "bigger on the inside") unless the context is clearly playful and earned
-   Exclamation marks more than sparingly — one per piece of short-form copy at most
-   Passive voice where active is possible
-   Vague CTAs ("click here", "learn more" with no context)

**Always:**

-   Write CTAs that describe the action and the benefit ("Listen to the episode", "Read the review", "See all Doctors")
-   Use Title Case for headings and sentence case for UI labels
-   Write meta descriptions as genuine summaries, not keyword dumps
-   Keep error messages human and specific — never "Something went wrong"

---

## Format guidance by content type

| Type                   | Approach                                                         |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Page headings          | Direct, specific, no pun unless it genuinely works               |
| CTAs                   | Action verb + context. Max ~5 words                              |
| Meta descriptions      | 140–155 characters. Accurate summary, no clickbait               |
| Intro copy             | Lead with what the page does for the reader, not what it is      |
| Social posts           | Conversational, platform-appropriate; no hashtag spam            |
| Guest outreach (email) | Warm, brief, fan-first; credentials mentioned once, not dwelt on |
| Guest outreach (DM)    | Even shorter, more casual; drop formality, keep warmth           |
| Error messages         | Specific, calm, tell the user what to do next                    |

---

## Doctor Who-specific conventions

-   Doctor incarnations: use ordinal numbers ("the Fourth Doctor", "the Twelfth Doctor") not actor names as primary reference, unless the context is specifically about the actor
-   Story titles: title case and italics in running copy where markdown/HTML allows (_Blink_, _Genesis of the Daleks_)
-   Big Finish: treat on equal footing with TV — it is not a lesser medium for this audience
-   Eras: Classic (1963–1989 plus the TV Movie 1996), Revival/New Who (2005–), Disney era (2023–) — use these consistently
-   Avoid the phrase "the TARDIS crew" unless quoting or in a clearly playful register

---

## What good looks like

**Weak:** "Dive into our amazing guide to every Doctor and find out all about their incredible eras!"
**Strong:** "Every Doctor, every era. What they brought to the role and where to start watching."

**Weak CTA:** "Click here to learn more"
**Strong CTA:** "Read the full review"

**Weak meta:** "Doctor Who podcast about classic and modern Who with reviews and interviews."
**Strong meta:** "The Big Blue Box Podcast covers every era of Doctor Who - reviews, interviews, and guides for fans old and new."
